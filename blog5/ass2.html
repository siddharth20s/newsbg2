Mercedes Brings First Level 3 Self-Driving Car To US

Feb 13, 2023

You read that correctly. Though Tesla is more commonly associated with self-driving vehicles, they only provide level 2 automation in the US. SAE International, formally known as the Society of Automotive Engineers, defines the different levels of driving automation in vehicles. Level 2 automation, which is commonly found in Teslas, provides automated driver support for steering, braking, and acceleration. However, the driver remains in control of the vehicle and must “constantly supervise” the car as it drives.
